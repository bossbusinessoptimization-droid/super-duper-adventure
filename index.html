2.4
Enable GitHub Pages (make site live)
~3 min
This flips the switch that makes your files visible as a real website.
  • In your repository, click the Settings tab (top right)
  • In the left sidebar, click Pages
  • Under "Branch", select main from the dropdown, then click Save
  • Wait 2–3 minutes, then refresh the page
  • You'll see a green banner: "Your site is live at https://yourusername.github.io/glowfuel"
  • Click that link to confirm your site is live ✅
☐
2.5
Connect your custom domain (glowfuel.com)
~15 min + 24hr DNS
This makes your site load at glowfuel.com instead of the github.io address. Requires two steps: one in GitHub, one in Namecheap.

Step A — In GitHub:

  • In GitHub Settings → Pages, find Custom domain
  • Type glowfuel.com and click Save
  • Check the box "Enforce HTTPS"

Step B — In Namecheap (point domain to GitHub):

  • Log into Namecheap → Domain List → Manage
  • Click Advanced DNS tab
  • Delete any existing A records
  • Add these 4 A records (copy exactly):
GitHub Pages DNS Records — paste these into Namecheap Advanced DNS
Type: A Record | Host: @ | Value: 185.199.108.153 Type: A Record | Host: @ | Value: 185.199.109.153 Type: A Record | Host: @ | Value: 185.199.110.153 Type: A Record | Host: @ | Value: 185.199.111.153 Type: CNAME | Host: www | Value: yourusername.github.io
⏰
DNS changes take up to 24 hours to fully propagate. Your site will work at the github.io address in the meantime.
